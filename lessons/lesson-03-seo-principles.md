# Lesson 03: SEO Principles in Writesonic

## Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

- Understand core SEO principles and how they apply to content creation
- Utilize Writesonic's SEO features to optimize content for search engines
- Conduct effective keyword research and implementation
- Optimize meta titles, descriptions, and headings for better search visibility
- Apply on-page SEO best practices to AI-generated content

## Key Topics

- **SEO Fundamentals Overview**
  - What is SEO and why it matters for business content
  - Search engine algorithms and ranking factors
  - Organic vs. paid search results
  - The role of user intent in SEO
  - Mobile-first indexing and page experience

- **Keyword Research and Strategy**
  - Understanding search volume, competition, and relevance
  - Long-tail vs. short-tail keywords
  - Using Writesonic's keyword suggestions and integration
  - Keyword mapping and content planning
  - Semantic keywords and LSI (Latent Semantic Indexing)

- **On-Page SEO Optimization**
  - Title tags: crafting click-worthy, SEO-friendly headlines
  - Meta descriptions: writing compelling search snippets
  - Header tags (H1, H2, H3) and content hierarchy
  - URL structure and optimization
  - Image alt text and optimization
  - Internal linking strategies

- **Content Optimization with Writesonic**
  - Using Writesonic's SEO checker and scoring
  - Keyword density and natural placement
  - Content length recommendations for target keywords
  - Featured snippet optimization
  - Schema markup considerations

- **Technical SEO Basics**
  - Page speed and performance
  - Mobile responsiveness
  - Crawlability and indexability
  - Canonical tags and duplicate content
  - HTTPS and site security

## Hands-on Activity/Assignment

**Assignment: SEO-Optimized Content Creation**

Select a business or industry you're familiar with and complete the following tasks:

1. **Keyword Research (Document your findings)**:
   - Identify 3-5 primary keywords relevant to your chosen business
   - Find 5-10 long-tail keyword variations
   - Document search intent for each keyword (informational, commercial, navigational, transactional)

2. **Create SEO-Optimized Blog Post (1,200-1,500 words)**:
   Using Writesonic, write a blog post that:
   - Targets your primary keyword
   - Includes natural placement of long-tail keywords
   - Uses proper heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3)
   - Contains optimized meta title and description
   - Includes internal linking opportunities (identify 3-5 places)

3. **SEO Analysis Report**:
   Write a brief report (300-400 words) that includes:
   - Your keyword strategy and rationale
   - How you optimized the content using Writesonic's SEO features
   - SEO score or metrics from Writesonic (if available)
   - Areas for improvement and next steps

**Deliverables:**
- Keyword research document
- Final blog post with SEO optimizations
- SEO analysis report

## Discussion Prompt

**Discussion Question:**

*"SEO is constantly evolving with algorithm updates and changing user behavior. How can businesses balance the need for SEO optimization with creating authentic, valuable content for readers? Discuss a time when you've encountered content that felt 'over-optimized' for SEO at the expense of readability. How would you use Writesonic to avoid this pitfall while still achieving strong search rankings?"*

Respond to this prompt (250-300 words) and engage with at least two classmates by sharing examples or alternative perspectives on balancing SEO with content quality.
